About the data

Weatherly aggregates public Earth-system datasets on one scrubbable timeline. Every layer is labeled by how trustworthy its claim is — not every signal on the globe means the same thing.

Epistemic classes

Each data layer carries one of five labels, shown as a badge next to the layer in the explorer.

Measured

Direct observation or instrument catalog. Earthquake locations, polar motion, ephemeris positions.

Modeled

Physics-based or reanalysis model output. ERA5 historical weather, IGRF geomagnetic field.

Derived

Computed index or coupling from measured inputs. Tidal index, Kp-estimated aurora oval.

Pedagogical

Teaching visualization — not a forecast or hazard product. The helical galactic-plane chart.

Exploratory

Hypothesis exploration only — not validated for prediction. Planetary alignment overlays.

Sources


IERS Earth Orientation Parameters C04 — measured

Polar motion and length-of-day. hpiers.obspm.fr

USGS Earthquake Catalog — measured

M≥5 global seismicity, incremental daily updates. earthquake.usgs.gov

Smithsonian GVP Eruptions Since 1960 — measured

Active eruption episodes, not the full Holocene volcano inventory. volcano.si.edu

JPL Horizons Ephemeris (DE441) — measured

Sun, Moon, and planetary positions. ssd.jpl.nasa.gov

Open-Meteo Historical Weather — modeled

ERA5 reanalysis archive at 16 reference cities. open-meteo.com

NOAA Storm Events Database — measured

US severe weather events. ncei.noaa.gov

NASA DONKI Space Weather — measured

CMEs, solar flares, and geomagnetic storm catalog. api.nasa.gov

NOAA IBTrACS — measured

Global tropical cyclone tracks. ncei.noaa.gov

PB2002 Plate Boundaries — derived

Bird (2003) digitization steps with kinematic class. Peter Bird / DeMets et al.

What this is not

Weatherly is a correlation instrument, not a prediction engine. USGS and seismology literature find no reliable prediction of individual earthquakes from lunar phase or planetary alignments. Tidal forcing is physically real; alignment-driven seismicity claims are exploratory only. Every panel in the explorer that carries an exploratory or pedagogical label says so directly.
